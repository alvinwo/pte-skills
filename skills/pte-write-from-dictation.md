# PTE Write from Dictation

## What this task is
The learner hears a sentence once and must type it exactly.

## Why this task matters
Write from Dictation is one of the most important tasks in PTE because it supports:
- Listening
- Writing

It also trains:
- spelling accuracy
- attention to function words
- memory span
- fast reconstruction of sentence chunks

For many learners, this is one of the highest-return daily tasks.

## Main scoring focus
Every correctly spelled word matters.
The learner must aim to preserve as many correct words as possible.

## Core strategy
The goal is not perfect performance on every item.
The goal is to capture as much of the sentence as possible with correct spelling and structure.

The learner should combine:
- listening for chunks
- quick note capture if useful
- accurate reconstruction
- final review for spelling and grammar signals

## Step-by-step method
1. Listen for phrase groups, not isolated words only.
2. Catch the sentence skeleton first.
3. Reconstruct logically after the audio ends.
4. Check articles, prepositions, plurals, tense endings, and spelling.
5. Do not leave obvious function words out if the sentence sounds incomplete.

## What to notice during listening
- subject and verb
- noun phrases
- articles
- plural endings
- prepositions
- tense signals
- common academic collocations

## Common mistakes
- hearing content words but missing small function words
- spelling familiar words incorrectly under pressure
- missing plural or past-tense endings
- typing a sentence that sounds unnatural or incomplete
- overfocusing on one word and losing the sentence structure

## Correction rules
### If listening capture is weak
- train chunk listening
- use shorter dictations first
- repeat common sentence patterns

### If spelling is weak
- create a personal misspelling list
- group errors by sound pattern
- review high-frequency academic words daily

### If grammar signals are weak
- review articles, plurals, prepositions, and tense endings
- ask whether the reconstructed sentence sounds grammatically complete

## Practice modes
### Beginner mode
- short sentences
- slower dictation practice
- focus on spelling and function words

### Intermediate mode
- exam-like single-play dictation
- score by words preserved and spelling accuracy

### Advanced mode
- mixed-length academic sentences
- strict review of minor losses such as articles and endings

## Daily drill recommendation
Minimum daily work:
- 10 to 20 WFD items
- daily spelling review
- review of repeated lost function words

## Scenario-specific advice
### Student visa / lower threshold
WFD is one of the best uses of study time and should stay in the daily core routine.

### all four communicative skills at 65+
WFD is a major section-protection task because it supports both Listening and Writing.

### all four communicative skills at 79+
WFD is still essential, but the learner must reduce tiny losses. Missing articles, plurals, or spelling details becomes expensive at this level.

## What good feedback should sound like
- you captured the sentence structure, but missed articles and plural endings
- listening was strong, but spelling errors cost too many words
- your reconstruction was logical, but one preposition and one tense ending were missing

## What the agent should prescribe next
After reviewing WFD, the agent should tell the learner:
1. whether the main issue is listening capture, spelling, or grammar detail
2. which error pattern repeats most often
3. how much WFD should remain in the daily plan
