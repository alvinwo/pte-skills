# PTE Respond to a Situation

Draft strategy page for the current PTE Academic question type **Respond to a Situation**.

## Source basis
This draft is based on the current official Pearson PTE Speaking & Writing format page checked on 2026-04-12.

Official details observed:
- Task: listen to and read a description of a situation, then answer a question about it with an extended spoken response
- Prompt length: text up to 60 words
- Skills assessed: speaking
- Time to answer: 40 seconds
- Preparation time: 10 seconds after the audio finishes

Official tip observed:
- Make good use of the preparation time
- Decide what main points to convey
- Consider whether a formal or informal register is needed
- Try to speak for the whole 40 seconds
- Avoid repeating ideas, correcting yourself too much, or hesitating

## What the task is really testing
This question seems to test whether the learner can:
- understand an everyday communicative situation quickly
- choose an appropriate tone and register
- respond clearly and naturally
- organize a short spoken message under time pressure
- keep speaking smoothly without long pauses

## Main scoring-oriented priorities
- understand the purpose of the situation
- choose the right tone: formal, polite, neutral, or friendly
- answer all parts of the prompt
- structure the response clearly
- speak continuously and understandably

## Best response structure
A very practical 4-step structure is:
1. Greeting or opening
2. Main purpose
3. Key supporting details or request / explanation / suggestion
4. Closing line

## Safe universal template
- Hello **[person / group]**,
- I am speaking because **[main purpose]**.
- I would like to **[request / explain / suggest / respond]** because **[reason]**.
- If possible, **[action or outcome wanted]**.
- Thank you for your time.

For informal situations, simplify the tone:
- Hi **[name]**,
- I wanted to talk about **[topic]**.
- The main issue is **[point]**.
- I think we should **[suggestion / action]**.
- Let me know what you think.

## Practical method
### During the prompt
- Identify who you are speaking to.
- Identify why you are speaking.
- Identify what outcome you want.
- Decide whether the situation needs a formal or informal tone.

### During the 10-second preparation time
- Pick the opening immediately.
- Plan 2 or 3 key points only.
- Decide one closing sentence.

### While speaking
- Start with the purpose, not with filler.
- Keep the tone consistent.
- Cover the full communicative goal.
- Keep speaking for most of the 40 seconds.
- If you make a small mistake, continue instead of restarting.

## Common situations this task may involve
- making a request
- apologizing
- explaining a problem
- asking for help
- giving a suggestion
- responding to a complaint
- confirming or changing a plan
- speaking to a teacher, colleague, classmate, customer, or manager

## Common mistakes
- choosing the wrong register
- sounding too casual in a formal situation
- forgetting the actual purpose of the message
- giving only one sentence and stopping too early
- using memorized phrases that do not fit the situation
- restarting after small mistakes

## Coaching advice for learners
- Treat the task like a short real-life voice message.
- Think **who + why + what outcome**.
- If unsure, choose a polite neutral tone.
- Use simple sentences if they help you stay fluent and clear.
- Formal accuracy matters more than advanced vocabulary.

## Drill ideas
- practice formal vs informal rewrites of the same situation
- answer everyday scenarios in 20, 30, then 40 seconds
- train with role prompts such as student-to-teacher or employee-to-manager
- practice giving one clear purpose sentence and two support sentences
- record and review whether the response actually matches the situation

## Agent behavior guidance
When coaching this task, the agent should:
- identify audience, purpose, and desired outcome
- suggest an appropriate register
- help the learner build a short spoken message with opening, purpose, support, and close
- penalize mismatch between tone and situation
- encourage clarity and completeness over fancy language
