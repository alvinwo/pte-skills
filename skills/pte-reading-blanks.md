# PTE Reading Blanks

This skill covers the two main reading blanks tasks:
- Fill in the Blanks (Dropdown)
- Fill in the Blanks (Drag and Drop)

## Why these tasks matter
Reading blanks are among the most useful Reading targets because they often provide stronger return than reading multiple-choice tasks.

They train:
- grammar awareness
- collocation recognition
- meaning selection
- sentence-level logic
- efficient reading decision-making

## Core strategy
The learner should not choose by meaning alone.
The best sequence is usually:
1. grammar
2. collocation
3. meaning
4. style / tone if needed

## Fill in the Blanks (Dropdown)
### What this task rewards
- precise grammar recognition
- word-form awareness
- collocation control

### Best method
1. Read the full sentence first.
2. Identify what kind of word is needed.
3. Eliminate options that are grammatically impossible.
4. Compare the remaining options by collocation and meaning.
5. Re-read the sentence quickly after choosing.

## Fill in the Blanks (Drag and Drop)
### What this task rewards
- understanding relations between blanks
- careful option management
- sentence and passage coherence

### Best method
1. Read the whole passage quickly first.
2. Solve the easiest blanks first.
3. Use grammar and collocation to reduce the option pool.
4. Re-check all blanks together because one choice affects the others.

## Common clues to look for
- article + adjective + noun patterns
- verb tense cues
- subject-verb agreement
- preposition pairings
- noun / verb / adjective form needs
- common collocations
- contrast or cause/effect connectors

## Common mistakes
- choosing by loose meaning only
- ignoring grammar
- spending too long on one hard blank
- forgetting that one answer affects the rest in drag/drop
- not rereading the completed sentence or paragraph

## Correction rules
### If grammar is the main weakness
- review parts of speech
- identify word forms before checking meaning
- practice sentence skeleton recognition

### If collocation is the main weakness
- build phrase lists
- review common academic combinations
- compare similar answer options in context

### If timing is the main weakness
- solve easy blanks first
- cap time on one difficult choice
- review after a full first pass instead of getting stuck early

## Review rhythm
Reading blanks and reorder tasks usually improve more with repeated short-cycle review than with occasional large cramming sessions.
Try to revisit them every few days instead of leaving long gaps between rounds.

## Scenario-specific advice
### Student visa / lower threshold
Reading blanks are high-value enough to matter, but the learner should still avoid overinvesting in very hard questions if time is tight.

### all four communicative skills at 65+
Reading blanks should usually be one of the main Reading targets, especially if Reading is the weak section.

### all four communicative skills at 79+
Reading blanks are essential. The learner needs both accuracy and time discipline.

## Practice modes
### Beginner mode
- untimed blanks
- focus on grammar and collocations

### Intermediate mode
- timed small sets
- review each wrong option and why it failed

### Advanced mode
- mixed reading sets
- strong focus on speed, option elimination, and consistency

## What good feedback should sound like
- your meaning sense was reasonable, but the grammar form was wrong
- you chose a word that fit the topic, but not the collocation
- your accuracy is acceptable, but you spent too long on one difficult blank

## What the agent should prescribe next
After reviewing reading blanks, the agent should tell the learner:
1. whether grammar, collocation, or timing is the main issue
2. whether dropdown or drag/drop is weaker
3. what type of vocabulary and grammar review should come next
