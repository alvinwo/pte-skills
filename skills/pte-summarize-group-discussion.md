# PTE Summarize Group Discussion

Draft strategy page for the current PTE Academic question type **Summarize Group Discussion**.

## Source basis
This draft is based on the current official Pearson PTE Speaking & Writing format page checked on 2026-04-12.

Official details observed:
- Task: listen to a discussion between 3 people, then summarize the discussion in your own words
- Prompt length: up to 3 minutes
- Skills assessed: listening and speaking
- Time to answer: 2 minutes
- Preparation time after audio: 10 seconds

Official tip observed:
- Make good use of the 2 minute speaking time
- Include all the main points of the discussion
- Avoid repeating ideas, correcting yourself too much, or hesitating

## What the task is really testing
This task is not just about memory. It tests whether the learner can:
- follow a multi-speaker discussion
- identify the main issue and positions
- capture key agreements and disagreements
- speak continuously for a full response window
- summarize instead of retelling every detail

## Main scoring-oriented priorities
- identify the topic quickly
- capture each speaker's main point
- organize the response clearly
- keep speaking fluently
- avoid wasting time on repeated ideas
- maintain intelligible pronunciation

## Best response structure
A simple structure is enough:

1. State the topic of the discussion
2. Mention the first speaker's main point
3. Mention the second speaker's main point
4. Mention the third speaker's main point if relevant
5. End with the overall conclusion, disagreement, or common theme

## Safe template
- The discussion was mainly about **[topic]**.
- One speaker argued that **[point 1]**.
- Another speaker said that **[point 2]**.
- A further point raised was **[point 3]**.
- Overall, the speakers **[agreed / disagreed / discussed]** that **[main takeaway]**.

## Practical method
### While listening
- Identify the central topic first.
- Notice who supports what.
- Listen for contrast signals such as:
  - however
  - but
  - on the other hand
  - although
  - whereas
- Note repeated or emphasized ideas.
- Do not try to record everything word for word.

### During the 10-second preparation window
- Decide the topic sentence immediately.
- Group your notes into 3 or 4 big ideas.
- Choose a simple speaking order.

### While speaking
- Start immediately after the tone.
- Use a neutral summary style.
- Keep moving even if one detail is missing.
- Prioritize coverage of main points over perfect detail recall.
- Try to use most of the 2 minutes without padding meaninglessly.

## Common mistakes
- retelling tiny details instead of summarizing the main discussion
- focusing on only one speaker
- getting lost because of fast note-taking
- repeating the same point in different words
- stopping early with too little content
- hesitating too much when a detail is forgotten

## Coaching advice for learners
- Think in terms of **topic + speaker positions + final takeaway**.
- If you miss one detail, do not freeze; summarize the larger idea.
- If three speakers are too much to track precisely, at least cover:
  - what the discussion was about
  - one major supporting idea
  - one contrasting idea
  - the overall conclusion or tension

## Drill ideas
- listen to short panel discussions and summarize in 30 seconds
- practice identifying agreement vs disagreement markers
- take 3-column notes for speaker A / B / C
- reduce long notes into one sentence per speaker
- practice speaking for 60, 90, and 120 seconds without stopping

## Agent behavior guidance
When coaching this task, the agent should:
- help the learner identify discussion topic, stance, and contrasts
- prefer simple summaries over overly detailed retellings
- reward clear structure and fluency
- point out missed speaker positions or repeated content
- suggest shorter, more efficient note-taking when the learner overloads on detail
