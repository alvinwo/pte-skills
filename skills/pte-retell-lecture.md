# PTE Retell Lecture

## What this task is
The learner listens to or watches a lecture, gets a short preparation window, and then retells the main content in spoken form.

## Why this task matters
Retell Lecture supports both:
- Listening
- Speaking

It also trains:
- note-taking
- content selection
- speaking fluency from notes
- summarizing under pressure

It is usually not as efficient as RS or WFD for many learners, but it still matters, especially for learners chasing stronger Speaking and Listening scores.

## Main scoring focus
- content
- pronunciation
- oral fluency

## Core strategy
The learner does not need to remember every detail.
They need to:
- identify the topic
- capture the major ideas
- speak continuously and clearly
- turn notes into a short organized response

A clean summary of the main lecture ideas is better than a detail-heavy broken answer.

## Safe response structure
1. Introduce the lecture topic
2. State the main idea or purpose
3. Mention 2 or 3 supporting points
4. End with a short overall takeaway

## Practical method
### While listening
- find the topic early
- note down keywords, not full sentences
- listen for repeated or emphasized points
- notice examples, but only keep them if they help the main message

### During preparation time
- organize notes into speaking order
- choose the strongest 2 or 3 points
- decide a clear first sentence

### While speaking
- start quickly
- use notes as support, not a script to panic over
- keep a steady pace
- do not stop to repair small content gaps

## Common mistakes
- trying to memorize everything
- taking too many messy notes
- spending too long on one example instead of the main point
- speaking in fragments instead of full connected ideas
- panicking after forgetting one detail

## Correction rules
### If content is weak
- focus on main ideas before examples
- train topic + 2 points + takeaway responses

### If fluency is weak
- shorten the note load
- practice speaking from keywords only
- avoid overlong sentences

### If notes are weak
- train abbreviation and keyword capture
- separate topic words from support words

## Practice modes
### Beginner mode
- short academic talks
- small note sets
- simple retell structure

### Intermediate mode
- timed note-taking and retell
- review whether the answer included the main idea

### Advanced mode
- exam-like lecture clips
- strict fluency and content review

## Scenario-specific advice
### Student visa / lower threshold
Do not overinvest here before RS, RA, DI, and WFD are stable.
Use RL as a support task, not the main engine.

### 4个7
Use RL if Listening or Speaking is weak, but usually after the biggest engine tasks are already in place.

### 4个8
RL becomes more important because integrated speaking and listening stability matter more. Focus on note quality, fluency, and less content loss.

## What good feedback should sound like
- you understood the lecture topic, but your notes led to a fragmented answer
- your fluency was good, but the response focused too much on an example instead of the main idea
- the structure was clear, but one key supporting point was missing

## What the agent should prescribe next
After reviewing RL, the agent should tell the learner:
1. whether the biggest issue is note-taking, content selection, or fluency
2. whether RL should stay as a support task or become a targeted weak-skill task
3. what note-to-speech drill should come next
