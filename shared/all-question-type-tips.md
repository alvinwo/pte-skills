# All Question Type Tips

This page captures practical PTE preparation tips across multiple question types. It is based on score-oriented study notes and is meant to preserve durable strategy rather than temporary question banks.

## Core philosophy
- Focus first on high-weight question types.
- Build methods that transfer across tasks.
- Prioritize accuracy, fluency, spelling, and controllable structure.
- Use prediction banks and recalled questions later as refreshable content, not as the foundation.

## Weight awareness
A practical preparation strategy should pay extra attention to the high-impact tasks.

### Listening
- WFD
- RS
- HIW
- FIB-L
- RL
- SST
- ASQ

### Writing
- WFD
- FIB-RW
- FIB-L
- WE
- SST
- SWT

The exact percentages can be refreshed later, but the key idea is stable: some tasks contribute far more than others, so prep time should reflect that.

## WFD — Write From Dictation
### Main idea
- Aim very high here because the task is heavily weighted.
- Treat WFD as both a listening and writing task.
- It can also support RS practice if trained the right way.

### Practical method
- Listen for meaning first, not only isolated words.
- Break the sentence into idea groups.
- If a word is hard to spell or easy to confuse, notice it and drill it repeatedly.
- Practice uncertain items again and again instead of writing every sentence every day.
- Try likely variants when unsure, especially:
  - articles
  - prepositions
  - singular/plural nouns
  - tense and voice

### Useful prep habit
- Re-listen to the items you could not fully reproduce.
- Use those misses as targeted review material.

## WE — Write Essay
### Main idea
- This is not the task that needs endless practice.
- A strong WE response is mainly about control.

### Priorities
- reliable template
- correct grammar
- correct spelling
- enough topic-related key words
- good word count control

### Practical method
- Memorize the template well.
- Improve typing speed.
- If you are unsure a word or structure is correct, replace it with something safer.
- Generate ideas from multiple angles around the topic:
  - politics
  - environment
  - economy
  - culture
  - education
  - health
  - family
  - community
  - daily life
- Aim for a safe word range, roughly around 250 to 300 words.

## SST — Summarize Spoken Text
### Main idea
- Understanding the lecture is more important than fancy structure.
- Content points matter, but grammar and spelling are critical.

### Practical method
- If notes are strong, build short simple sentences around the captured content.
- If notes are weak, use very simple framing sentences to organize the response.
- Make sure the answer stays in the required word range.
- Do not exceed the word limit.
- Focus on capturing several core keywords or short phrases rather than trying to reconstruct everything.

### Safe fallback structure
- The speaker was discussing ...
- Firstly, he or she said that ...
- Later, he or she pointed out that ...
- Moreover, he or she talked about ...
- Finally, he or she suggested that ...

## SWT — Summarize Written Text
### Main idea
- This is a high-value scoring opportunity and should be treated carefully.

### Practical method
- Do not simply combine any two sentences.
- Identify the main idea of each paragraph.
- Try to include as many paragraph-level main points as possible.
- Use commas and simple connectors like and / but.
- Keep strictly within the word limit.

### Format discipline
- Start with a capital letter.
- End with one final period.
- Use commas in the middle.
- Remove examples, extra description, and unnecessary adjectives/adverbs if the sentence is too long.

## FIB-RW — Reading & Writing Fill in the Blanks
### Main idea
- This is a pattern-recognition task.
- Grammar, collocation, meaning, and sentence role all matter.

### Practical method
- Before reading the whole passage deeply, inspect the blank and predict what type of word is needed.
- Ask whether the blank is testing:
  - tense
  - voice
  - word form
  - grammar function
  - meaning / collocation
- If it is grammar-focused, reduce the sentence to the core structure and inspect the role of the blank.
- If it is vocabulary-focused, learn the phrase or usage in context and save the mistake for review.
- Watch for:
  - active vs passive
  - time markers such as since
  - perfect tenses
  - modal or hypothetical structures
  - synonym / antonym clues in nearby context

## RS — Repeat Sentence
### Main idea
- This is not only a memory task. It is also a fluency task.
- Understanding helps recall.

### Scoring awareness
- Fluency and pronunciation matter a lot.
- Content is important, but you do not need a perfect sentence to get useful credit.

### Practical method
- Practice by listening and repeating many real-style sentences.
- Save the items you did not understand or reproduce well, then cycle them again.
- Focus on understanding the sentence and repeating meaningful chunks.
- Prioritize common multi-word phrases because they can recover content quickly.
- Do not get stuck on one unfamiliar word and lose the rest of the sentence.
- When repeating, do not imitate the audio unnaturally. Deliver it more like controlled Read Aloud with sensible pauses.

## RL — Retell Lecture
### Main idea
- Similar to SST, understanding the content matters more than a fancy template.

### Practical method
- A template can help you start, but it should not create hesitation between the template and your own content.
- Review likely lecture topics and key points so the content feels familiar.
- Keep transitions simple and smooth.

## FIB-L — Listening Fill in the Blanks
### Main idea
- Concentration is crucial.

### Practical method
- Use efficient navigation between blanks.
- On the first pass, write something recognizable rather than chasing perfect spelling immediately.
- Still pay attention to singular/plural and verb form.
- After the audio, audit every blank quickly.

## ASQ — Answer Short Question
### Main idea
- This can be easy score if prepared lightly but consistently.

### Practical method
- Review common short-answer items without overinvesting time.
- Mark the ones you do not know and revisit them every few days.
- If you are unsure between two likely answers, a combined answer may sometimes still preserve a keyword hit.
- Keep answers short and direct.

## HIW — Highlight Incorrect Words
### Main idea
- This is mostly an attention-control task.

### Practical method
- Follow the audio closely.
- If you catch a mismatch, respond quickly and then move on.
- If you are uncertain, do not overthink and overclick, because wrong clicks can hurt.

## Study process recommendations
- Do official mock exams before the real test.
- Use the mocks to decide whether you are already at target score level.
- Spend more time on high-weight tasks than low-weight ones.
- Review mistakes in cycles rather than treating every practice item equally.
