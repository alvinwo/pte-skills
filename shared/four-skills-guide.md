# Four Skills Guide

Use this file for broad reading, speaking, listening, and writing advice.

## Reading
- prioritize blanks first
- use grammar first, meaning second, collocation third
- do not overspend time on low-yield MCQ
- review repeated mistakes closely and often

## Speaking
- fluency and control matter more than sounding fancy
- warm up before serious RS work
- keep structures simple in DI, RL, SGD, and RTS
- do not pause too long after starting

## Listening
- WFD and RS are core engine tasks
- HIW is mostly attention and pace control
- FIB-L needs concentration, rough note cues, and fast cleanup after the audio
- if you miss one detail, recover quickly and keep going

## Writing
- keep sentences simple and accurate
- word count and format discipline matter
- spelling and grammar mistakes are expensive
- use safe structures rather than complicated ones

## Essay note
For WE, do not overinvest compared with bigger engine tasks.
Use a reliable structure, choose a side quickly, and keep examples broad and simple.
