# Vocabulary Strategy

This file explains how PTE learners should study vocabulary in a way that actually improves scores.

Use it when the learner says:
- I need to 背单词
- how should I study vocabulary for PTE?
- what words matter most?
- how can vocabulary help RS / RA / WFD / reading blanks?

## Core principle
PTE vocabulary study should not be only random memorization of isolated difficult words.

The best vocabulary system for PTE combines:
1. **recognition** — understanding words when listening or reading
2. **recall** — producing words in speaking or writing
3. **spelling** — writing words correctly under pressure
4. **pronunciation** — saying common words clearly enough for speaking tasks
5. **collocation** — knowing what words naturally go together

## Vocabulary categories to prioritize

### 1. Academic connectors
These help writing and speaking structure.

Examples:
- however
- therefore
- moreover
- in addition
- as a result
- on the other hand
- for example
- in conclusion

### 2. High-frequency academic topic words
These help across DI, RL, SGD, RTS, WE, SWT, and SST.

Topic groups:
- education
- technology
- environment
- health
- work and employment
- society and culture
- government and policy
- business and economy
- science and research

### 3. High-frequency collocations
Examples:
- make a decision
- take responsibility
- play an important role
- have a significant impact
- gain experience
- face challenges
- achieve success
- improve performance

### 4. Spelling-sensitive words
These are useful for WFD, SST, and general accuracy.

Examples of categories to review:
- words with silent letters
- plural endings
- common academic nouns
- commonly confused spellings
- function words that are easy to miss

### 5. Pronunciation-sensitive words
These matter especially for RA and RS.

Examples of focus areas:
- past tense endings
- plural endings
- word stress
- reduced forms in connected speech
- pairs that are easy to confuse when listening

## Best way to study vocabulary for PTE

## Layer 1: Learn by topic
Group words by common exam topics instead of alphabetical lists.

Example groups:
- education: curriculum, assessment, academic, literacy, tuition
- technology: innovation, digital, device, software, automation
- environment: pollution, climate, conservation, sustainable, emission
- health: nutrition, disease, treatment, physical, mental

## Layer 2: Learn in phrases, not only single words
A learner remembers and uses language better in chunks.

Better:
- significant increase
- sharp decline
- long-term effect
- practical solution
- public transport system

Not just:
- significant
- decline
- effect
- solution
- transport

## Layer 3: Study spelling and pronunciation together
The same word should be learned as:
- meaning
- spelling
- pronunciation
- example phrase

This is especially useful because PTE is integrated.
A word may appear in reading, be heard in listening, and be needed in writing or speaking.

## How vocabulary supports specific question types

## RA
Useful vocabulary study for RA means:
- recognizing common academic words instantly
- pronouncing common word patterns clearly
- practicing stress and chunking

## RS
Vocabulary helps when the learner:
- quickly recognizes familiar words
- stores phrases as chunks instead of isolated words
- becomes less likely to panic when hearing academic content

## DI / RL / SGD / RTS
Vocabulary helps by providing:
- safe reporting verbs
- comparison language
- trend language
- cause/effect language
- opinion and suggestion language

Examples:
- the image illustrates
- the graph shows
- there is a clear increase
- the main point is that
- one possible solution is
- the speakers mainly discuss

## Reading blanks
Vocabulary matters most when combined with grammar and collocation.
The learner should ask:
1. which part of speech fits?
2. which meaning fits?
3. which collocation sounds natural?

## WFD
Vocabulary helps through:
- spelling accuracy
- recognition of frequent function words
- recognition of common academic phrases

## WE / SWT / SST
Vocabulary should support clear, safe, error-controlled output.
The learner does not need fancy words if they create mistakes.

---

## Vocabulary mistakes to avoid

### Mistake 1: memorizing only rare hard words
This often gives poor return.
High-frequency academic words and collocations are more useful.

### Mistake 2: learning words without pronunciation
This hurts RA and RS.

### Mistake 3: learning words without spelling
This hurts WFD, SST, and writing accuracy.

### Mistake 4: learning words without example phrases
This hurts DI, WE, and blanks.

### Mistake 5: trying to sound too advanced
For many learners, safe correct vocabulary is better than fancy risky vocabulary.

## Practical daily vocabulary routine

### 10-minute version
- 3 min review old words
- 3 min learn 5–10 new topic words
- 2 min review collocations
- 2 min spelling or pronunciation check

### 20-minute version
- 5 min spaced repetition review
- 5 min new topic words
- 5 min collocations and example phrases
- 5 min dictation, pronunciation, or short sentence production

### 30-minute version
- 10 min review
- 10 min new words and topic clusters
- 5 min pronunciation work
- 5 min writing or speaking use

## Recommended output formats for learners
The skill should encourage learners to store vocabulary like this:

### Format A: word card
- word
- meaning
- pronunciation note
- example phrase
- example sentence

### Format B: topic table
- topic
- key nouns
- key verbs
- key adjectives
- key phrases

### Format C: error-driven list
- word misspelled in WFD
- correct spelling
- sound pattern
- new example phrase

## Coaching rule
When a learner says they need to 背单词, do not just tell them to memorize more.
Tell them:
1. which vocabulary categories matter most for their target
2. how to review them daily
3. how to connect vocabulary to question types
4. how to avoid wasting time on low-value word study
