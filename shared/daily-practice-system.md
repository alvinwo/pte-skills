# Daily Practice System

This file defines the default daily and weekly practice rhythm for PTE learners.

Use it when the learner asks:
- what should I do every day?
- how should I divide my week?
- what are the non-negotiables?
- what should I practice if I only have 1 hour per day?

This page should work together with:
- `shared/scenario-based-study-plans.md`
- `shared/current-score-priority-matrix.md`
- `shared/target-score-planning.md`

## Core principle
Daily practice should not feel random.

Every learner plan should contain four layers:
1. **daily engine tasks** that drive score gain
2. **weak-skill blocks** that target the current bottleneck
3. **maintenance tasks** so stronger skills do not collapse
4. **review loops** so mistakes are actually reduced over time

## Default daily non-negotiables
These are the tasks most learners should do almost every day.

### Speaking engine
- RA
- RS

### Listening and writing engine
- WFD
- RS

### Language foundation
- vocabulary review
- spelling review for high-frequency words
- pronunciation / fluency correction

## Why these are non-negotiable
### RA
Builds:
- oral fluency
- pronunciation
- confidence with microphone delivery
- phrase chunking

### RS
Builds:
- memory span
- sentence chunking
- listening discrimination
- speaking fluency under pressure

### WFD
Builds:
- listening precision
- spelling accuracy
- function-word awareness
- writing accuracy

These are not just question types.
They are foundational drills that transfer across the exam.

## Daily schedule templates by available study time

## Option A: 45–60 minutes per day
For busy learners or lower-threshold targets.

### Suggested structure
1. 10 min vocabulary + spelling review
2. 10 min RA
3. 10 min RS
4. 10 min WFD
5. 10–20 min weak-skill block

### Weak-skill block examples
- Reading weak -> FIB Reading or RO
- Listening weak -> HIW or FIB-L
- Speaking weak -> DI
- Writing weak -> SWT / WE sentence control

## Option B: 90 minutes per day
For moderate targets or 4个7 learners.

### Suggested structure
1. 15 min vocabulary review
2. 15 min RA
3. 15 min RS
4. 15 min WFD
5. 15 min weak-skill block A
6. 15 min weak-skill block B or review log

## Option C: 2–3 hours per day
For strong 4个7 pushes, 4个8 targets, or urgent deadlines.

### Suggested structure
1. 20 min vocabulary + spelling + collocations
2. 20 min RA
3. 25 min RS
4. 25 min WFD
5. 25 min DI / speaking expansion
6. 25 min reading blanks / listening blanks / HIW depending on weakness
7. 20 min writing quality block or timed integrated task
8. 20 min error review and correction

## Scenario-specific daily systems

## Student visa / lower-threshold learner
### Goal
Pass efficiently, not perfectly.

### Daily priorities
- RA every day
- RS every day
- WFD every day
- one short weak-area block
- vocabulary every day

### Weekly emphasis
- 4–5 days of core engine practice
- 2–3 days of mixed sets
- light writing structure work
- minimal time on low-yield MCQ unless obviously weak

### Warning
Do not waste too much time polishing difficult low-yield tasks if the score target is modest.

## 4个7 learner
### Goal
Protect every section floor.

### Daily priorities
- RS every day
- WFD every day
- RA most days
- one targeted weak-skill block every day
- vocabulary every day
- short review of repeated mistakes

### Weekly emphasis
- 2–3 mixed timed sets
- 1–2 section-level mini-mocks
- more time on the weakest communicative skill
- protect strong skills with light maintenance

### Warning
Do not focus only on the overall feeling of improvement.
Track whether each section is actually stable enough to hit target.

## 4个8 learner
### Goal
Raise ceiling and reduce leaks.

### Daily priorities
- RS every day
- WFD every day
- RA every day
- DI very frequently
- vocabulary every day
- spelling and pronunciation cleanup every day
- error log review every day

### Weekly emphasis
- several timed mixed sets
- at least one high-quality mock or section simulation each week
- deliberate work on low-yield leak prevention
- repeated review of small accuracy problems

### Warning
At this level, careless errors matter a lot.
The learner does not only need more practice; they need cleaner practice.

## Weekly structure template

## Day types
Use three kinds of study days.

### 1. Engine day
Main goal: strengthen the highest-yield core tasks.

Typical tasks:
- RA
- RS
- WFD
- DI or blanks

### 2. Weakness day
Main goal: attack one weak communicative skill.

Typical tasks:
- Reading weakness -> FIB + RO + timing control
- Listening weakness -> WFD + HIW + FIB-L
- Speaking weakness -> RA + RS + DI + fluency correction
- Writing weakness -> WFD + SWT + WE + spelling control

### 3. Mixed simulation day
Main goal: test stability under time pressure.

Typical tasks:
- mixed question set
- mini mock
- review of errors immediately after completion

## Recommended weekly rhythm
### Lower-threshold learner
- 3 engine days
- 2 weakness days
- 1 mixed simulation day
- 1 lighter day or rest / review day

### 4个7 learner
- 2 engine days
- 3 weakness days
- 1 mixed simulation day
- 1 review and repair day

### 4个8 learner
- 2 engine days
- 2 weakness days
- 2 mixed simulation days
- 1 repair and cleanup day

## How to review practice properly
Practice without review is one of the biggest wastes of time.

After each serious session, the learner should log:
- what question type was practiced
- what errors repeated
- whether the errors were content, fluency, pronunciation, spelling, grammar, timing, or strategy
- what single correction rule should be used next time

### Good correction rules
- slow down slightly at punctuation in RA
- keep RS answers smooth even if one word is missed
- in DI, always state the chart type and main trend early
- in WFD, check articles, plural endings, and verb tense before finalizing
- in reading blanks, decide by grammar first, meaning second, collocation third

## What the agent should output when giving a plan
A useful daily plan should always tell the learner:
1. what to do every day
2. how many minutes to spend on each block
3. what their weak-skill block is
4. what they can skip or reduce
5. what they must review after practice
