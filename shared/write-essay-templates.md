# Write Essay Templates

This file collects practical PTE essay templates that are easy to remember, easy to adapt, and usually long enough when expanded with topic-specific points.

## Selection principles
These templates are chosen because they are:
- simple to memorize
- logically structured
- flexible across many topics
- easy to keep grammatically correct
- helpful for reaching a safe word count

## General advice
- Do not memorize one full essay word for word.
- Memorize structure and sentence patterns instead.
- Use a template only as a frame; fill it with topic-specific key points.
- Prefer clear, correct sentences over impressive but risky language.

## Template 1: Direct opinion template
Best for prompts like:
- To what extent do you agree or disagree?
- Do you think this is a positive or negative development?

### Structure
1. Introduction
2. Reason 1
3. Reason 2
4. Short conclusion

### Template
**Introduction**  
It is widely argued that [topic]. In my opinion, I [agree/disagree/partly agree] with this view because [reason category 1] and [reason category 2] are highly important.

**Body paragraph 1**  
The first reason is that [main idea]. From a practical perspective, [example or effect]. This is especially true in terms of [education/work/daily life/social change].

**Body paragraph 2**  
Another important point is that [main idea]. Although some people argue that [opposite view], I believe that [response]. As a result, [broader consequence].

**Conclusion**  
In conclusion, I believe that [position]. While the opposite argument has some value, [topic] should mainly be understood from the perspective of [main takeaway].

## Template 2: Balanced-but-clear template
Best for prompts where both sides are easy to discuss.

### Structure
1. Introduction with clear position
2. Advantages / supporting side
3. Disadvantages / opposing side
4. Conclusion with final judgment

### Template
**Introduction**  
The issue of [topic] has become increasingly important in modern society. Although there are arguments on both sides, I believe that [main position].

**Body paragraph 1**  
On the one hand, supporters of [side A] argue that [benefit 1]. In addition, [benefit 2]. This can have a positive impact on [group or area].

**Body paragraph 2**  
On the other hand, critics point out that [drawback 1]. They may also argue that [drawback 2]. Even so, these disadvantages do not outweigh the overall benefits because [judgment].

**Conclusion**  
To conclude, both sides present reasonable arguments, but I am more convinced that [main position]. Therefore, [short final statement].

## Template 3: Cause-effect-impact template
Best for prompts about trends, changes, and developments.

### Structure
1. Introduce trend and opinion
2. Causes
3. Effects / impacts
4. Conclusion

### Template
**Introduction**  
In recent years, [trend] has become a common feature of modern life. In my view, this change is mainly caused by [cause] and has significant effects on [area].

**Body paragraph 1**  
One major cause of this trend is [cause 1]. Another contributing factor is [cause 2]. Together, these factors have changed the way people [behavior].

**Body paragraph 2**  
The effects of this development can be seen in several areas. For example, it influences [education/work/family life/culture]. While this trend creates [benefit], it may also lead to [drawback].

**Conclusion**  
Overall, [trend] is the result of several social and technological changes. Its long-term influence will depend on how people and institutions respond to it.

## Template 4: Problem-solution template
Best for prompts asking what should be done.

### Structure
1. Introduce the problem
2. Explain why it matters
3. Suggest solutions
4. Conclude

### Template
**Introduction**  
[Problem] has become a serious issue in many parts of the world. In my opinion, this problem should be addressed quickly because it affects [group/area] in important ways.

**Body paragraph 1**  
One reason this issue is serious is that [reason 1]. Furthermore, [reason 2]. If no action is taken, the situation may become worse in the future.

**Body paragraph 2**  
Several measures can be taken to solve this problem. First, [solution 1]. Second, [solution 2]. These steps would help reduce the negative impact of [problem].

**Conclusion**  
In conclusion, [problem] requires immediate attention. With effective action from individuals, institutions, and governments, this issue can be managed more successfully.

## Easiest recommended template
If the learner wants one default structure for most topics, use this:

### Simple 4-part default
**Introduction**  
It is often argued that [topic]. I [agree/disagree/partly agree] with this opinion because it affects [area 1] and [area 2] in significant ways.

**Body paragraph 1**  
The first important point is that [main point]. For example, [supporting idea]. This shows that [mini conclusion].

**Body paragraph 2**  
Another key point is that [main point]. Although some people believe that [opposite idea], I think that [response]. Therefore, [result].

**Conclusion**  
In conclusion, I believe that [position]. This is because [reason summary], and these factors have an important influence on modern life.

## How to make the essay long enough
To safely reach a good word count, expand each body paragraph with:
- one topic sentence
- one explanation sentence
- one example sentence
- one result or implication sentence

## Safe phrase bank
Use phrases like:
- it is often argued that
- in my opinion
- the first important point is that
- another key point is that
- for example
- this shows that
- on the other hand
- as a result
- in the long term
- in conclusion

## Good default paragraph plan
A reliable target is:
- introduction: 2 to 3 sentences
- body paragraph 1: 4 to 5 sentences
- body paragraph 2: 4 to 5 sentences
- conclusion: 2 sentences

## Final note
The best template is not the most advanced one. The best template is the one a learner can use quickly, accurately, and consistently under time pressure.
