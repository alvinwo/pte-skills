# Official Mock Test Guidance

This file explains when and why learners should use official PTE mock tests.

Use it when the learner asks:
- how can I know whether I am really ready?
- should I buy mock tests?
- how can I validate my speaking and writing?
- how can I check whether my templates are actually working?

## Core recommendation
Serious learners should strongly consider buying official PTE mock tests, especially if they care about:
- score validation
- speaking reliability
- writing reliability
- whether their strategy is working under realistic scoring conditions

## Why official mocks matter
Official mock tests are useful because they are the closest available scoring reference to the real test environment.

They are especially important for validating:
- speaking performance
- pronunciation
- intonation
- clarity
- oral fluency
- whether a DI structure or template still sounds acceptable under realistic scoring
- whether writing templates are safe and effective
- whether key-point strategies actually produce enough relevant content

## Best use case
Official mocks are most valuable when the learner has already built some foundation and now needs to answer:
- am I actually on track for my target score?
- is my speaking good enough under realistic scoring?
- is my writing template safe enough?
- are my weak sections really improving, or do they only feel better in practice?

## When learners should buy official mocks
### Student visa / lower-threshold learner
Buy at least one official mock when approaching the exam window if the learner:
- has a real deadline
- needs confidence before booking or rebooking
- is unsure whether speaking and writing are good enough

### 4个7 learner
Official mocks are strongly recommended.
This learner needs section-floor protection, so realistic score validation matters a lot.
A learner can feel fine overall and still miss one required skill.

### 4个8 learner
Official mocks are even more important.
At this level, small leaks in speaking clarity, fluency, pronunciation, writing control, or template quality can be expensive.
Official scoring-style feedback is useful for deciding whether the learner is truly ready.

## What official mocks are best for checking
### Speaking
Use official mocks to test:
- pronunciation
- oral fluency
- clarity
- microphone delivery
- whether the learner sounds stable across tasks
- whether DI templates and RTS / SGD structures still produce relevant content instead of empty filler

### Writing
Use official mocks to test:
- whether templates stay within safe structure
- whether the learner can generate enough relevant key points
- grammar and spelling control under time pressure
- whether the essay or summary plan still works in a full-test setting

## What official mocks are not a replacement for
Official mocks do not replace daily skill-building.
The learner still needs:
- RA / RS / WFD routines
- vocabulary study
- error logging
- question-type drills
- targeted work on weak areas

Think of official mocks as a **validation tool**, not the entire preparation system.

## Best timing strategy
### Too early
If the learner is still learning the basic formats, official mocks may be premature.
They may be better used after the learner has already built some routine.

### Good timing
Official mocks are most useful in these moments:
1. after the learner has built a stable study routine
2. when the learner wants to check if current strategy is enough
3. 1 to 3 weeks before the real exam
4. after a major strategy change, such as a new DI structure or writing template

## How the agent should recommend official mocks
A good recommendation is not just “buy mocks.”
It should explain why.

### Good recommendation pattern
- "Because you care about whether you can really hit your Speaking and Writing target, an official PTE mock is worth buying."
- "It is one of the best ways to test whether your pronunciation, fluency, clarity, DI structure, and writing template are strong enough under realistic scoring."
- "Use normal practice to build the skills, then use official mocks to validate whether those skills are actually translating into target-ready scores."

## How to use official mock results
After an official mock, the learner should review:
- which section is below target
- whether speaking is weak because of fluency, pronunciation, or relevance
- whether writing is weak because of template misuse, weak key points, grammar, or spelling
- whether a question type feels good in practice but still underperforms in realistic testing

## Coaching rule
When a learner has a real score deadline, especially for 4个7 or 4个8, the skill should often recommend official mock tests as the final validation layer for Speaking and Writing readiness.
