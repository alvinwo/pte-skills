# Write Essay Strategy Draft

This draft captures a practical, score-oriented approach for PTE Write Essay.

## Main philosophy
A strong PTE essay does not need to be highly original. It needs to be:
- on topic
- long enough
- grammatically controlled
- correctly spelled
- logically organized

## Recommended approach
1. Use a familiar template.
2. Hit the required word count.
3. Keep grammar simple and correct.
4. Avoid spelling mistakes.
5. Generate key points quickly by expanding the topic outward.
6. Prefer easy, well-structured templates that can be reused across many prompts.

See also: `shared/write-essay-templates.md`

## Suggested template
### Introduction
- Paraphrase the topic.
- State your position clearly.
- Mention the main areas you will discuss.

### Body paragraph 1
- Present your strongest reason.
- Explain it with one or two supporting examples.

### Body paragraph 2
- Present another reason or discuss the opposite side briefly.
- Show comparison, limitation, or wider consequence.

### Conclusion
- Restate your position.
- Summarize the main idea in a concise way.

## Brainstorming model
### Step 1: Start from the center
Identify the exact topic words.

Example:
- digital media
- books
- significance in everyday life

### Step 2: Move outward in circles
Think about effects on:
- individuals
- students
- workers
- families
- communication habits
- learning habits
- entertainment
- public culture
- technology adoption
- policy or institutional change

### Step 3: Look from both sides
Even if you support one side, collect ideas for:
- advantages
- disadvantages
- short-term impact
- long-term impact
- social consequences
- cultural consequences

## Example key points for the digital media vs books topic
### Advantages of digital media
- instant access to information
- lower distribution cost
- portability and convenience
- easier sharing and updating
- broader reach across devices

### Advantages of books
- deeper reading focus
- fewer distractions
- stronger cultural and academic value
- better long-form comprehension for some readers
- physical ownership and archival value

### Wider discussion areas
- education
- lifestyle change
- screen dependence
- social behavior
- technological progress
- cultural preservation
- political influence of media channels

## Useful score-friendly phrases
- it is widely believed that
- this issue remains highly relevant today
- I partly agree with this view
- there are several reasons for this
- the most obvious benefit is
- another important point is that
- however, this does not mean that
- from a broader social perspective
- in educational settings
- in the long term
- therefore, it can be argued that

## Agent behavior guidance
When helping learners with WE topics, the skill should:
- suggest a side quickly if the learner hesitates
- generate key points from multiple categories
- offer reusable sentence patterns
- highlight possible scoring keywords and phrases
- prioritize accuracy over sophistication
- use fill-in-the-blank templates when helpful
- keep templates flexible instead of prescribing every blank too tightly
